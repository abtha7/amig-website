About Our Organization

The Aerospace Medicine Interest Group is dedicated to exploring the fascinating intersection of medicine and aviation. We bring together medical students, faculty, and professionals interested in the unique challenges and opportunities in aerospace medicine.

What We Do

  • Host guest speakers and clinical educators
  • Discuss current topics in aerospace medicine
  • Explore career pathways and opportunities
  • Connect students with mentors and research opportunities
  • Build a community of aerospace medicine enthusiasts

Aerospace Medicine

The medical science of flying safely in the aerospace environment. Aerospace medicine encompasses the prevention and treatment of physiological and medical conditions unique to aviation, including issues related to altitude, acceleration, hypoxia, and spatial disorientation.

Career Pathways in Aerospace Medicine

Aerospace medicine offers two distinct career routes. Hover over each to learn more and explore resources:

Civilian Route
Aviation Medical Examiner positions, commercial airline support, and civil aviation consulting with flexible scheduling.
Key Areas:
  • Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) certification
  • Private practice & aeromedical facilities
  • Commercial airline medical departments
  • Pilot medical evaluations
  • Civil aviation consulting

Your Path to Aerospace Medicine

This timeline outlines the typical milestones and actions you should consider at each stage of medical school and beyond to build toward a career in aerospace medicine.

1st Year
  • Join AMIG and start building connections
  • Explore aerospace medicine basics through interest group meetings
  • Focus on core medical science coursework
  • Consider shadowing an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME)
1st Year Summer
  • Research summer research opportunities in aerospace physiology
  • Explore NASA internship programs
  • Network with faculty and mentors in aerospace medicine
2nd Year
  • Complete foundational clinical rotations
  • Attend AMIG guest speaker sessions
  • Start exploring residency program options
  • Consider pursuing AME certification
2nd Year Summer
  • Conduct summer research project in aerospace medicine
  • Apply for NASA summer internships
  • Attend aerospace medicine conferences (ASMA, ASGSR)
3rd Year
  • Complete clinical rotations with aerospace medicine focus if available
  • Research military vs civilian pathways
  • If military interested: explore HPSP programs early
  • Network at national conferences
3rd Year Summer
  • Final research or rotation opportunities
  • Strengthen residency application materials
  • Secure strong letters of recommendation
4th Year
  • Apply for NASA aerospace medicine clerkship (April or October)
  • Submit aerospace medicine residency applications
  • Complete interviews with residency programs
  • Match into residency program
Post-Medical School
  • Complete internship year (required before residency)
  • Enter aerospace medicine residency (2-3 years depending on program)
  • Complete Master's degree (usually integrated into residency)
  • Board certification in aerospace medicine
Note: This timeline is a guide. Your specific path may vary based on interests, opportunities, and career goals. Discuss your plans with AMIG officers and faculty mentors!

Resources & Opportunities

Aerospace Medicine Residency Programs

There are currently five accredited U.S. residency programs in aerospace medicine. All require completion of an MD/DO and internship year before enrollment.

Civilian Programs

University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB) - Galveston, TX
Two-year program with clinical rotations supporting commercial airlines, NASA centers, and government agencies.
Learn More
Mayo Clinic - Rochester, MN
Two-year fellowship with training at USAF School of Aerospace Medicine, FAA, NASA, and flight training included. Includes Master's in Public Health.
Learn More

Military Programs

Air Force - Wright-Patterson AFB, OH
Three-year program (1 academic + 2 practicum). Includes Master's in Public Health.
Learn More
Navy - Pensacola NAS, FL
Specialized aviation medicine training for naval aviators and aircrew.
Learn More
Army - Fort Novosel, AL
36-month program with Master of Public Health from University of West Florida.
Learn More

Post-Residency Opportunities

Additional specialization and leadership opportunities available through fellowships and advanced certifications.

Space Medicine Fellowships
Two-year programs for Emergency Medicine or other specialty residents interested in clinical care and research in space medicine operations.
