About Our Organization

The Aerospace Medicine Interest Group is dedicated to exploring the fascinating intersection of medicine and aviation. We bring together medical students, faculty, and professionals interested in the unique challenges and opportunities in aerospace medicine.

What We Do

  • Host guest speakers and clinical educators
  • Discuss current topics in aerospace medicine
  • Explore career pathways and opportunities
  • Connect students with mentors and research opportunities
  • Build a community of aerospace medicine enthusiasts

Join Us

Whether you're curious about aviation physiology, interested in becoming an aerospace medicine physician, or simply want to learn more about this specialized field, we welcome you! Contact one of our officers to get involved.

Aerospace Medicine

The medical science of flying safely in the aerospace environment. Aerospace medicine encompasses the prevention and treatment of physiological and medical conditions unique to aviation.

Resources & Opportunities

Career Routes in Aerospace Medicine

Aerospace medicine offers two primary career pathways for physicians. Each has distinct benefits, training requirements, and lifestyle considerations.

Civilian Route
  • Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) certification
  • Work in private practice or aeromedical facilities
  • Conduct pilot medical evaluations
  • Telemedicine and remote medical support
  • Civil aviation consulting
  • Commercial airline medical positions
  • Flexible scheduling and location options
Military Route
  • Air Force Aerospace Medicine residency
  • Flight surgeon positions
  • Military pilot and aircrew medical support
  • Advanced training in hypoxia and G-force physiology
  • High-altitude and pressure suit expertise
  • Combat aviation medicine
  • Active duty commitment with structured advancement

Research Opportunities

Interested in conducting research in aerospace medicine? Below are opportunities for students to get involved in meaningful medical research that bridges aviation and healthcare.

NASA Clerkships

NASA Johnson Space Center - Houston, TX
Four-week intensive aerospace medicine clerkship for 4th-year medical students. Offered twice annually in April and October. Includes lectures on aerospace medicine topics, pilot physiology, medical certification, and tours of NASA JSC facilities.
Learn More
NASA Kennedy Space Center - Florida
Clerkship available for 4th-year medical students and residents in aerospace or occupational medicine. Accept medical residents in other fields with aerospace medicine or occupational health interests. Hands-on experience with space medicine and astronaut health.
Learn More

Additional Research Areas

Aviation Medical Examiner Shadowing
Shadow an Aviation Medical Examiner to learn about medical certification of pilots and the practical aspects of aerospace medicine in clinical practice.

Hypoxia & Altitude Research
Participate in research studying the physiological effects of altitude and hypoxia. Great opportunity to learn about research methodology and respiratory physiology.

Spatial Disorientation & Vestibular Research
Study the vestibular system and spatial disorientation in aviation contexts. This research has direct clinical applications.

National Organizations & Conferences

Aerospace Medical Association (AsMA)
The primary organization for aerospace medicine professionals and students. Publishes the journal Aviation, Space, and Environmental Medicine.
Visit AsMA
AMSRO (Aerospace Medicine Student & Resident Organization)
Official student and resident organization of AsMA. Provides mentorship, networking, and education opportunities.
Visit AMSRO
AsMA Annual Scientific Meeting - May
Premier annual conference featuring presentations on cutting-edge research, clinical updates, and networking opportunities.
Learn More
ASGSR (American Society for Gravitational and Space Research)
Organization focused on biological and physical sciences research in space. Annual conference showcases research in gravitational biology and bioastronautics.
Visit ASGSR
